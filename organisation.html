Organisation

ORGANISATION

DIFAG has a Board of Trustees and an Advisory Council that comprise highly reputable development-oriented individuals from Africa. There is a President while the International Council also contributes to the organization through utilizing their contact and wealth of experience to improve on DIFAG’s work. The Secretariat implements the decisions made by the Board/Council.

There are programme officers and volunteers who assist the Executive Director in the day-to-day running of the Secretariat.

OLAOLU A. ADEWUMI

Dr. Olaolu Adewale Adewumi, a Management Consultant, Trainer, Researcher and Minister of God, attended St. Andrew’s College of Education, Oyo, Nigeria where he bagged Nigerian Certificate in Education (NCE) in Business Education. He proceeded to the Imo State University, Owerri and obtained a Bachelor of Science (B.Sc) Honours degree in Government and Public Administration. He also studied for a Post-Graduate Diploma in Public Administration (PGDPA) at the University of Ibadan.

Dr. Adewumi obtained a Master’s Degree in Public Administration (MPA) from the Adekunle Ajasin University, Akungba Akoko, Nigeria. He later studied Divinity at the Word of Faith Bible College and Christian Education at the Kingdom Life University, USA and bagged Doctor of Divinity (D.D) and Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D) in Divinity and Christian Education respectively.

NWOKOCHA MARIAN NKECHI

Mrs. Marian Nwokocha Nkechi, a Management Consultant, educationist and technocrat, holds multiple degrees in Education and Management Sciences. She is highly interested in youth education, skill development and overall progress of Africans at home and the Diaspora.

FUNMI ADESIYAN

An educationist of repute, attended the Obafemi Awolowo University and University of Ado-Ekiti, Nigeria where she studied Biology and Chemistry Education. She is passionate about the girl-child education and entrepreneurship development for women and youths.

MEDINAT BELLO

Mrs. Medinat Bello studied Agricultural Science Education at the Federal College of Education, Kontagora, Niger State, Nigeria where she obtained the Nigerian Certificate in Education (NCE). She proceeded to the University of Uyo where she read Guidance and Counselling and graduated with Bachelor of Arts (B.A) Honours. Mrs. Bello is keenly interested in seeing to the emancipation of Africans (especially women) from the shackles of poverty and hopelessness.

INTERNATIONAL ADVISORY COUNCIL

PRESIDENT - MR. ALIEU K. JARJU, THE GAMBIA

Alieu K Jarju, DIFAG President is the Director-General, Management Development Institute (MDI), The Gambia. He had held various positions in the MDI including Deputy Director General and Head of Computer Department before becoming the Chief Executive Officer of the national institution. Mr. Jarju also serves in the University of The Gambia Senate.

Mr. Jarju is genuinely keen in human capacity building for the promotion of sustainable development and emancipation from the shackles of poverty and despondency and has brought in keen interest in seeing to the realization of the vision of DIFAG

ELIZABETH E. SCHREMBS - GERMANY

Chief Mrs Elizabeth E. Schrembs, Chairman of the Nigeria community in Germany is a woman of substance who devotes her life to the service of mankind.

Mrs Schrembs interest is to see a new and better Africa with progress, peace and prosperity in all ramifications.

J. O. ANJORIN

Pa J. O. Anjorin, Chairs the Governing Council of DIFAG. An astute technocrat, Pa. J. O. Anjorin retired from the services of the Administrative Staff College of Nigeria (ASCON), Topo – Badagry as Director of Studies/Head of Department many years back. Pa Anjorin is a tired-less individual who is highly committed to the progress of Africans at home and in the diaspora.

DR. AYO ADERINWALE - CHAIRMAN, Board of Patrons, NIGERIA

Aderinwale was born in Osogbo, Nigeria. He attended the University of Lagos for his first degree in Political Science and a Master of Science in Political Economy and International Relations in 1985 and 1987, respectively. He is an alumnus of the United Nations University's International Leadership Academy, and of Harvard University's Executive Programme for Leaders in Development.

He provides consulting services for several international agencies, including the United Nations, European Union and the former Organization of Africa Unity now known as the African Union. He participated in the initial drafting of the Millennium Plan for Africa (MAP) which later became the New Partnership for Africa's Development (NEPAD) and was an inaugural member of the Nigeria Steering Committee of the Africa Peer Review Mechanism (APRM).

He is currently the Chairman of the Board of Directors of Justrite Limited, one of the largest superstores in Lagos and Ogun States; Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Business School Netherlands and Chairman of the Board of Governors of the Bells Educational Services, as well as the Bells Comprehensive Secondary School for Boys and Girls.

As the Executive Director of ALF, he began several programs such as the Regional African Parliamentarians Conference, the Africa Women's Forum, the Legislative Internship Programme and the Democratic Leadership Training Workshop. He helped create the Conference on Stability, Security, Development and Cooperation in Africa (CSSDCA) which was adopted by the African Union in 2002.

Presently he is the deputy chief coordinator at the Olusegun Obasanjo Presidential Library while Chief Obansanjo remains the chief coordinator.

On 21 December 2006, the President of Nigeria, Olusegun Obasanjo, conferred on him the membership of the Order of the Federal Republic. His classmates and colleagues launched a foundation, the Ayodele Aderinwale Foundation for Education and Leadership in Africa (AAFELA) in his honor, which provides scholarships to young boys and girls in schools across the country.

PROFESSOR OLUKAYODE AKINYEMI

Prof. Olukayode had his PhD IN SOLID EARTH Physics from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria, after which he proceeded to the Pontifical University of Curitiba, Brazil for his postdoctoral research. He joined the department of Physics, Federal University of Agriculture (FUNAAB) in 1998 and became a professor in 2013. He was appointed the Head of Department of Physics in 2008, and in 2012 he became the Director of research Grants Management, whose main task was to mobilize grants and provide grants-writing supports. He is presently the Head of Hydrology and climate Change Program, a World Bank SPONSORED Africa Centre of Excellence, FUNAAB. He was a visiting scientist, Ecohydrology Department , Wageningen University and Research Centre, Netherlands in 2012. He was the Association of African Universities Visiting Professor to the Department of Earth Sciences, University of Legon in 2016. He is a fellow and member to many professional bodies. He has over sixty scientific publications and has attended many national and international conferences and workshops.

Prof. Akinyemi is very passionate about human emancipation and elevation of living standard.

VOLUNTEERS/INTERNS

DIFAG welcomes Volunteers and Interns from all walks of life who signify interest in its work. There are always vacancies for more Interns and Volunteers to assist in the ever-increasing volume of work.

  2. Information on areas of possible assistance for Africans to take advantage of;

  4. Invitation to international programmes;
